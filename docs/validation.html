Evaluation with Digital Twin Practitioners

Replication package attached in the blue box.

Replication Guidelines

This section provides the results of the evaluation conducted with Digital Twin domain experts. The evaluation details, including all responses collected from the online questionnaire, are available in the replicationpackage.zip .

If you encounter any issues or need further clarification, please do not hesitate to contact us.

The final validation aimed to evaluate whether TwinArch is complete, useful, and easy to apply across various Digital Twin systems. Respondents evaluated three key hypotheses:

  • Completeness (H1): Does TwinArch cover all essential elements?
  • Usefulness (H2): Does it provide practical value for Digital Twin design and development?
  • Perceived Usability (H3): Is it intuitive and easy to apply in practice?

The responses were captured using a Likert scale, with detailed visualizations shown below.

Figure: Likert plot summarizing completeness, usefulness, and perceived usability.

Key Findings

  • Completeness: The majority of respondents (65–80%) agreed or strongly agreed that TwinArch is complete. Practitioners highlighted its comprehensive architectural coverage and emphasized its potential to address the current lack of unified Digital Twin definitions.
  • Usefulness: 45–55% of respondents strongly agreed that TwinArch provides practical value, particularly for fostering collaboration between stakeholders and guiding development through well-structured guidelines.
  • Perceived Usability: Although generally positive, perceived usability received slightly lower scores, with practitioners pointing out challenges related to practical examples and customization for domain-specific applications.

Figure 1: Box plots comparing online survey responses.

Statistical and Practical Significance

To further analyze the responses, a Wilcoxon signed-rank test was conducted to determine the statistical significance of the hypotheses. The results confirmed that the responses were significantly positive across all views and attributes.

Figure 2: Wilcoxon test results.

Additionally, the practical significance of the findings was evaluated using Cohen’s effect size measure, which indicated a large effect across all attributes. Completeness and usefulness were especially notable, while perceived usability, though slightly lower, still demonstrated strong practical utility.

Figure 3: Effect size showing practical significance of responses.
